About
I am an assistant professor of computer science at San Francisco State University. In the past, I have been fortunate to work with Professor Jean-Baptiste Tristan (at Boston College), Professor Dawn Song (at UC Berkeley), and Professor Greg Morrisett for my PhD (at Harvard).
I am interested in studying and building the next-generation programming language abstractions and systems that we need to accelerate science research. The importance of this research comes from the confluence of two trends: (1) it is becoming increasingly costly to run scientific experiments in the lab to gain scientific knowledge and (2) it is becoming increasingly complex to leverage advances in computer science and hardware to run computational simulations that may alleviate these costs.
My research group and I tackle this challenge by building programming languages and systems with a focus on applications in computational chemistry (which is critical in the study of climate science) and mathematics. Our work draws on techniques from programming languages, compilers, and machine learning.
