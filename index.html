About
I am an assistant professor of computer science at San Francisco State University. In the past, I have been fortunate to work with Professor Jean-Baptiste Tristan (at Boston College), Professor Dawn Song (at UC Berkeley), and Professor Greg Morrisett for my PhD (at Harvard).
I am interested in language-based techniques for expressing artificial intelligence (e.g., probabilistic programming). Why mix programming languages with artificial intelligence? Because programming languages provide the bridge between expression of functionality in software and its execution on hardware, and so it provides a way to understand artificial intelligence that separates the what from the how. I am particularly interested in scientific applications of this technology (e.g., scientific machine learning).
To scratch this itch, I teach classes on programming languages, artificial intelligence, and quantum computing. My research group also studies a language-based approach to artificial intelligence.
