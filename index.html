About
I am an assistant professor of computer science at San Francisco State University. In the past, I have been fortunate to work with Professor Dawn Song (at UC Berkeley) and Professor Greg Morrisett for my PhD (at Harvard).
I am interested in education and research at the intersection of programming languages and artificial intelligence (e.g., probabilistic programming and neuro-symbolic AI). This approach provides a way to understand artificial intelligence that separates the software (i.e., the world of bits/symbols) from the hardware (i.e., the world of atoms).
I run the Language-Based Artificial Intelligence (LBAI) Lab at San Francisco State. I teach classes on artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and formal models and semantics.
