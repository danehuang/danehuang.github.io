Programming & Intelligent Systems (P&IntSys)
The central question we study is: "what should be programmed in order to build an intelligent system?" At one extreme, we can explicitly program intelligent behavior via logical rules. At the other extreme, we can program a learning algorithm that learns intelligent behavior directly from data. We tackle this question by building intelligent systems with a focus on applications in natural sciences and mathematics. Our work draws on techniques from programming languages theory and machine learning.
Research
  • Intelligent Systems for Natural Sciences. How can AI and programming languages technology push the state-of-the-art in modeling atomistic systems (e.g., molecules, materials)? Properties of these systems can be studied computationally using ab-initio (i.e., from first-principles quantum mechanics) solvers. However, these solvers can be quite compute-intensive to run. Projects involve developing new models, algorithms, and systems for accelerating atomistic modeling.
  • Intelligent Systems for Mathematics. Can neural networks reason about formal mathematics? Formal mathematics is mathematics done in a way that a computer can check that the proof is correct. Unfortunately, they are difficult to construct and it often takes much time and effort on the part of human experts. Projects involve building and training AI systems to do reasoning tasks: algebraic rewrites, euclidean geometry, construct proofs in a proof assistant.
